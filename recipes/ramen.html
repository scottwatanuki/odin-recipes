Miso Ramen

Description

Miso ramen is a Japanese noodle soup. It is called Miso Ramen because its broth is made from miso along with chicken stock, vegetables, and ground pork. This kind of ramen soup is thick and rich. The noodles should be fresh and are best when springy, chewy, and yellow. Miso toppings can be chashu, menma, corn, vegetables, and butter.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup corn
  • 1 egg, soft-boiled
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1/2 pouch miso stock
  • 1 clove garlic, smashed
  • 3/2 inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 3/2 chopped green onions
  • 1 chicken breast
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste
  • 1 serving ramen noodles
  • Nori, for garnish

Steps

  1. Melt the butter in a pan and fry up your corn until warm. Put it to one side.
  2. Soft-boil your eggs and blanch the spinach in hot water. You can also put this to one side for now.
  3. In a big pan, add your chicken stock, scallions, garlic, and ginger, and boil it all up for 30 minutes. Add in the chicken and let it all simmer together for another 20 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, you can get cooking fresh ramen noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Remove the chicken (check it's cooked through) and set it to one side.
  6. Add the miso paste into the broth and let it dissolve.
  7. Shred the chicken and you are ready to go.
  8. Divide the noodles between the bowls, pour over the broth. Add the spinach, corn, shredded chicken, scallions, boiled eggs, and nori.
  9. Enjoy!
